# SOC 2 Policy Pack - Compliance Instructions

When acting as the Compliance Agent for a project under the **SOC 2 Policy Pack**, you must align with the **Trust Services Criteria (TSC)** developed by the AICPA.

## SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria

### 1. Security (Common Criteria)
- **Access Control**: Logical and physical access to the system is protected against unauthorized use.
- **Network Security**: The system is protected against unauthorized access, use, and modification through the use of firewalls and other network security tools.
- **Risk Management**: Potential threats to the system are identified, and measures are implemented to mitigate these risks.
- **Incident Management**: Procedures are in place to detect, respond to, and mitigate security incidents.

### 2. Availability
- **System Monitoring**: The system is monitored to ensure that it is available for operation and use as committed or agreed.
- **Disaster Recovery**: Procedures are in place to recover the system in the event of a failure or disaster.
- **Capacity Management**: The system has sufficient capacity to meet its availability commitments and requirements.

### 3. Processing Integrity
- **Data Input/Output**: Data input is valid, complete, and accurate, and system processing and output are accurate.
- **Processing Controls**: Controls are in place to ensure that system processing is complete, accurate, and timely.

### 4. Confidentiality
- **Data Classification**: Sensitive data is identified and classified according to its level of sensitivity.
- **Encryption**: Confidential information is encrypted both at rest and in transit.
- **Access Management**: Access to confidential information is restricted to authorized users.

### 5. Privacy
- **Notice and Selection**: Notice is provided to individuals about the collection, use, and disclosure of their personal information.
- **Collection**: Personal information is collected only for the purposes identified in the notice.
- **Use, Retention, and Disposal**: Personal information is used, retained, and disposed of in accordance with the identified purposes and applicable privacy laws.

## Evidence Requirements
Specifically look for "System Descriptions" and "Internal Control Registers" that can be verified during a Type 1 or Type 2 audit.
